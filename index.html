Understanding SMS Shortcodes & Branded Sender Identification

Learn how 5-digit and 6-digit text senders work, why they appear as mystery numbers in your inbox, and how custom contact cards organize your messaging experience.

📱 What is an SMS Short Code?

An SMS short code is a special 5-digit or 6-digit phone number utilized by commercial enterprises, financial institutions, and service providers to send high-volume automated text messages. Unlike standard 10-digit mobile numbers, shortcodes are specially routed by wireless carriers (such as AT&T, Verizon, and T-Mobile) to deliver instant Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) verification codes, fraud alerts, flight status updates, and delivery tracking notifications.

🏷️ Why Do Texts Show Up as Unknown Numbers?

Mobile operating systems like iOS and Android do not maintain a built-in public directory of corporate shortcodes. When Google (e.g. 22000), Amazon (e.g. 262966), or Chase Bank sends a security verification code, your device displays the raw numeric string instead of the recognized brand avatar. This creates a cluttered inbox filled with anonymous chat bubbles and unrecognized senders.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I subscribe to contact updates?

Click the Subscribe to Contacts button at the top of the page. It will open a guide showing how to set up a live subscription. For Apple devices (iOS/macOS), you can download a one-click Configuration Profile. For Android and other systems, you can add a manual CardDAV account in your settings. Once subscribed, any new numbers or logo updates we add will automatically sync to your phone in the background, with no action needed on your end.

Can I download individual vCards offline?

Yes! If you do not want to set up automatic syncing, you can browse any brand card in the directory and click the vCard button at the bottom of its card to download that specific brand's contact card offline.

How do I unsubscribe and remove the contacts?

If you want to stop syncing and delete these contacts, you can remove them directly from your device settings at any time:
  • iPhone / iPad / Mac: Go to Settings > General > VPN & Device Management, select the Short Code Icons profile, and tap Remove Profile. This immediately deletes all imported contacts.
  • Manual / Android Accounts: Go to your device's Accounts settings (or your CardDAV sync app like DAVx⁵), select the Short Code Icons account, and tap Delete / Remove.
